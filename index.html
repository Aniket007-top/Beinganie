Track 01

Artificial Intelligence

AI teaches machines to recognize patterns, understand language, make decisions, and learn from data. This pathway covers AI basics, ML, DL, neural networks, computer vision, NLP, LLMs, and responsible deployment.

Beginner Level

AI for school learners

Teaching style: Grade 10 teacher, simple language, relatable examples, and visual thinking.

  • What is AI: Making computers act smart using data or rules.
  • Machine Learning: Learning from examples instead of writing every rule by hand.
  • Deep Learning: A powerful part of ML using many layered artificial neurons.
  • Neural Networks: Layered math systems inspired by how brain connections learn patterns.
  • Computer Vision: Lets machines understand images, faces, roads, and objects.
  • NLP: Lets machines understand text, translation, chat, and speech.
  • Everyday AI: Recommendations, spam filters, maps, voice assistants, fraud alerts.

Story Example

Showing many cat pictures to a computer is like teaching a child with examples. Over time it learns what a cat usually looks like.

Intermediate Level

Technical AI foundations

Teaching style: PGT teacher, technical vocabulary, working principles, and practical projects.

  • Supervised learning: Train on labeled examples.
  • Unsupervised learning: Discover hidden patterns without labels.
  • Reinforcement learning: Learn through reward and penalty.
  • Workflow: Collect data, clean it, split it, train, validate, test, deploy.
  • Evaluation: Accuracy, precision, recall, F1, confusion matrix.
  • Core models: Regression, trees, forests, SVMs, CNNs, RNNs, transformers.
  • Mini projects: Sentiment analysis, digit recognition, house price prediction.

Learn by Doing

Pick a real problem and write its input data, output prediction, and how you would measure success.

Advanced Level

Engineer-level AI systems

Algorithms, internals, architectures, deployment pipelines, and future directions.

  • Neural internals: Weighted sums, activations, gradient descent, backpropagation.
  • CNNs: Use convolutions and pooling to learn image features.
  • Transformers: Self-attention models relationships between tokens in parallel.
  • LLM pipeline: Tokenization, embeddings, attention blocks, pretraining, fine-tuning, inference.
  • MLOps: Data versioning, experiments, deployment, drift monitoring, feedback loops.
  • Responsible AI: Bias, fairness, privacy, explainability, governance.
  • Future scope: Multimodal AI, edge AI, efficient local models, human-AI collaboration.

Track 02

Internet of Things

IoT connects physical objects to digital networks so they can sense, share, and act on data. This track covers sensors, microcontrollers, communication protocols, smart systems, cloud-edge architectures, security, and scalable deployments.

Beginner Level

IoT made simple

Teaching style: school-level explanation with simple examples from homes, farms, schools, and cities.

  • What is IoT: Devices with sensors and internet connection that send or receive data.
  • Sensors: Temperature, light, motion, distance, gas, humidity, pressure.
  • Actuators: Parts that do something like switch a light, move a motor, or open a valve.
  • Microcontrollers: Small computers like Arduino and ESP32 that read sensors and control outputs.
  • Connectivity: WiFi, Bluetooth, RFID, Zigbee, LoRa, and cellular.
  • Everyday uses: Smart bulbs, fitness trackers, weather stations, smart farming, asset tracking.
  • Main idea: Sense, send, decide, act.

Simple Example

A smart plant system checks soil moisture. If the soil is dry, it turns on a pump automatically.

Intermediate Level

Connected systems and architecture

Teaching style: more technical, with architecture layers, protocols, and project planning.

  • Device layer: Sensors, actuators, microcontrollers, firmware logic.
  • Network layer: MQTT, HTTP, CoAP, BLE, WiFi, LoRaWAN.
  • Platform layer: Brokers, databases, analytics, dashboards, alerts.
  • Edge computing: Process data near the device to reduce latency and bandwidth.
  • Case studies: Smart home energy monitoring, predictive maintenance, crop monitoring.
  • Mini projects: Temperature logger, motion-based alert, smart parking counter.
  • Security basics: Identity, authentication, encryption, safe firmware updates.

Learn by Doing

Sketch an IoT system with four blocks: sensor, controller, network, and dashboard. Label what data moves between them.

Advanced Level

Engineer-level IoT systems

Scalable architectures, embedded software, protocols, edge AI, and industrial-grade design.

  • IoT architecture: Perception, transport, processing, application, and business layers.
  • Embedded systems: Real-time sensing loops, interrupts, low-power modes, modular firmware.
  • Protocol selection: Balance range, energy, reliability, bandwidth, and cost.
  • Scalability: Device provisioning, telemetry pipelines, digital twins, fleet management.
  • Edge AI: Running compact ML models on ESP32, microcontrollers, SBCs, or gateways.
  • Industrial applications: Smart grids, logistics, healthcare monitoring, Industry 4.0.
  • Future scope: TinyML, satellite IoT, federated learning on edge devices, sustainable sensing.

Track 03

Robotics

Robotics combines mechanics, electronics, sensing, software, AI, and control systems to make machines interact with the physical world. This track explains components, robot types, automation, navigation, control, and real-world applications.

Beginner Level

Robotics for first-time learners

Simple teaching style with examples from vacuum robots, drones, factory arms, and delivery bots.

  • What is a robot: A machine that senses, thinks, and acts in the real world.
  • Main components: Structure, motors, sensors, controller, power source, and software.
  • Motors: DC, servo, and stepper motors create movement.
  • Sensors: Ultrasonic, infrared, encoders, IMU, cameras, and touch sensors.
  • Controllers: Arduino, ESP32, Raspberry Pi, STM32, and PLCs.
  • Types of robots: Industrial, mobile, drones, humanoids, medical, and educational robots.
  • Applications: Manufacturing, rescue, surgery, warehouses, farming, homes.

Simple Robot Story

A line-following robot uses sensors to detect a path and motors to stay on it, just like a learner following road markings.

Intermediate Level

Control and movement principles

Teaching style: technical but accessible, focusing on system behavior, feedback, and design examples.

  • Kinematics: Study of position, speed, and motion.
  • Dynamics: Explains how forces and torque affect movement.
  • Feedback control: Sensors measure state and the controller corrects errors.
  • PID control: Proportional, integral, and derivative correction.
  • Localization: Knowing where the robot is using sensors or maps.
  • Path planning: Finding a safe route from start to goal.
  • Projects: Obstacle avoidance robot, pick-and-place arm, self-balancing bot.

Learn by Doing

For a delivery robot, list what it must sense, how it decides, and what it needs to move safely.

Advanced Level

Engineer-level robotics systems

Autonomy, mechatronics, software architecture, safety, and modern robotic intelligence.

  • Robot architecture: Perception, localization, planning, control, actuation, safety.
  • Manipulation: Forward and inverse kinematics, grippers, force control, trajectories.
  • Mobile autonomy: SLAM, sensor fusion, occupancy grids, route planning.
  • Software stack: ROS/ROS 2 nodes, topics, services, actions, simulation.
  • Human-robot interaction: Safety, trust, explainability, ergonomics.
  • Industry uses: Warehouse robots, surgical systems, collaborative arms, rovers, assistive devices.
  • Future scope: Soft robotics, swarms, bio-inspired systems, AI-guided autonomy.
